# Analysis of Negative Feedback in Usage Reflections

Based on a keyword analysis of the **671** `usage_reflection` responses in `bootcamp.db`, I have identified **17 distinct learners** who submitted explicitly negative feedback regarding either their experience with the course format, the AI tools, or the broader impact of AI.

## Statistical Overview
- **Total `usage_reflection` responses analysed:** 671
- **Responses flagged for negative keywords:** 55
- **Distinct learners providing validated negative feedback:** 17 (~2.5% of all course reflections, or ~7.7% of the unique learner base responding to this quiz)

The negative feedback falls into three main categories:
1. **Course Structure & Learning Curve (Overwhelm & Accessibility)**
2. **Frustration with AI Tool Performance (Errors, hallucinations, image generation)**
3. **Philosophical and General Suspicion of AI (Scepticism, data privacy, bad actors)**

---

## 1. Negative Feedback About the Course & Learning Curve

These learners felt overwhelmed by the pace, the format of the classes, or their inability to grasp when to use which tool.

> **Learner 160 (Course 48)**
> "I haven't used AI this week. To be honest, I am struggling to get into the course. Generally, online learning is extremely difficult for me to get on with. I can't keep up with all the IT jargon, the slides and the talking over the slides whilst I'm trying to read them. I find there is too much detail, or the same thing is said several times, but in slightly different ways. I need headlines and a small amount of detail. I could probably do with breaking out into a room and reviewing what we have done so far, as I feel I've probably missed 50% of the content so far."

> **Learner 62 (Course 52)**
> "This week has been a lot of trial and error and mostly error unfortunately... anything that requires a diagram has been a complete disaster. I'm also trying to get my head around which tool to use for which job and getting a bit confused. I haven't had much opportunity with each tool so I feel like I haven't grasped what to use and when. A bit overwhelmed this week."

> **Learner 58 (Course 49)**
> "I have a better understanding following taking notes and watching the various videos you suggested, the 27 min was a bit fast for me... I am an anxious learner and feel I need to go through everything thoroughly in order to understand, slow thinker. Also trying to keep things in order and not be overwhelmed."

> **Learner 73 (Course 52)**
> "I think I got slightly confused with the SOP and GEM, and that the SOP isn't always required in order to create a GEM... I'm finding it hard to see how AI can help me with tasks."

> **Learner 202 (Course 49)**
> "I tried to write a project / SOP to support this and asked ai to create a mood board - without success - my instructions were not clear enough and ai was confused and got stuck ."

## 2. Frustration with Specific AI Tools

These learners hit the limits of what current AI can do, running into hallucinations, image glitches, or frustrating UI loops.

> **Learner 162 (Course 48)**
> "Loading this all into Studio AI and getting it to build the App has proved frustrating. Weirdly the geo location works but some of the other content that I've asked it to incorporate was wildly wide of the mark... So my feeling is that it likes putting things in just to tick a box… it actually doesn't have integrity. It is very keen but boy do you have to keep on top of it."

> **Learner 10 (Course 52)**
> "I also experimented with creating an animated gif which didn't work well, I think I need to create a more detailed prompt. Interesting I tried to do it in a rush and the results were bad."

> **Learner 120 (Course 49)**
> "My colleague used ChatGPT, Gemini, and Copilot to generate a team photo as we haven't got an up to date professional one - but it wasn't very successful - kept changing our faces!"

> **Learner 195 (Course 52)**
> "The logos were less than impressive but interesting. We then asked for a mascot to be generated and it just decided to give us a man holding a drawing... We end up with carton scenes with a carton fox in a hard hat with a random logo for our brand on it. All in all not the best but certainly an interesting learning curve"

I've basically given up. I've gotten some beautiful images of octopi with mostly 9 tentacles. And the LLMs can count the tentacles accurately and still the image engines draw 9."

> **Learner 125 (Course 51)**

## 3. General Scepticism, Ethics, and AI Impact

These learners brought negative feelings or concerns about AI's larger implications and its misuse by others.

> **Learner 64 (Course 48)**
> "I've become far more aware of poor and irresponsible uses of AI, particularly online. Badly implemented AI, especially in advertising and marketing, genuinely frustrates me... That kind of use not only devalues genuine communication but also undermines trust in AI more broadly. Seeing AI used to flood feeds with shallow, formulaic content or to push questionable claims without accountability is something I find difficult to ignore"

> **Learner 147 (Course 52)**
> "I'm a massive AI sceptic - which is important because I was surprised by how much I enjoyed using it!... The implications concern me but it did come up with a great blog I am not putting onto my website."

> **Learner 34 (Course 52)**
> "I also still feel some resistance to AI , not because I don't see the benefit but because of the scare mongering on line and people pushing their political agendas, its hard to know what is true and what isn't."

> **Learner 17 (Course 51)**
> "Previously whilst self-employed I lost a lot of work to AI in a similar vein to what was discussed this morning AI detectors causing a lot of headaches. I was writing freelance for several companies and have done successfully for many years, but with AI and AI detectors they started challenging my authenticity which was super frustrating. When you add to that other companies who feel the AI is not an issue and are therefore generating their own content and blogs without needing to pay a writer, I came to a bit of a sticky point"

> **Learner 135 (Course 51)**
> "I was a bit concerned about the anonymisation of data So I switched project... Having gained reassurance about the security of data in the google environment i will go ahead and carry on"
