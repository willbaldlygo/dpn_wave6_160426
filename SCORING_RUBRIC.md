# Layer 1 Scoring Rubric: Learner Progression

This rubric is used to rate learner development across the bootcamp based on their subjective, self-reported open-text responses. 

## The Objective
To systematically quantify string responses to understand if learners moved from mere awareness or early experimentation to active, embedded integration of AI tools in their daily work.

## The Four Dimensions (Scored 1-3)

### Dimension 1: AI Adoption Level
*How embedded is AI in the learner's working life?*
- **1:** No use, or heard of it but not using it.
- **2:** Occasional or experimental use of one or two tools.
- **3:** Regular use embedded in workflow; described as a working tool instead of a novel experiment.

### Dimension 2: Specificity of Application
*How concrete and detailed are the learner's descriptions of AI use?*
- **1:** Vague intentions only (e.g., "I want to save time" or "I want to learn AI").
- **2:** Named tools or broad task types (e.g., "I use ChatGPT for emails").
- **3:** Concrete, implemented examples with specific use-cases (e.g., "I built an Airtable base linked to Zapier to automate client intake").

### Dimension 3: Reported Business/Work Impact
*Has AI produced tangible results in their work?* **(Note: Score as N/A at baseline)**
- **1:** No impact described, or only hypothetical anticipated impact.
- **2:** General impact described (e.g., "it's saving me time" or "I feel more confident").
- **3:** Specific tangible impact named — for Entrepreneurs this means business growth, revenue generation, or measurable time saved. For Employed learners, this means gaining new leadership/responsibilities or creating new workflows for the team.

### Dimension 4: AI Self-Awareness
*Does the learner show a critical understanding of how AI works and how to use it safely and effectively?*
- **1:** No evidence of critical understanding; treats AI as a black box or magic tool.
- **2:** Shows a general understanding of AI's role and some basic limitations.
- **3:** Demonstrates nuanced understanding — deliberately selects appropriate tools for specific tasks, mentions human-in-the-loop reviews, cites specific prompting techniques (like Chain-of-Thought), or clearly understands when the AI output cannot be trusted.

---

## Question Mapping by Learner Track

Because the Entrepreneur and Employed tracks ask slightly different questions, they map to the dimensions differently.

### For BASELINE (Both Tracks)
- **D1:** Q4 ("Do you or your business currently use AI?")
- **D2:** Q4 + Q5 combined
- **D3:** N/A (Hasn't happened yet)
- **D4:** Q5 + Q7 (Entrepreneur: "What skills do you need?") OR Q6 (Employed: "What skills do you need?")

### For OUTCOMES (Entrepreneur Track)
- **D1:** Q5 ("How will skills impact your business / what impact felt?")
- **D2:** Q7 ("Specific examples of actions taken")
- **D3:** Q8 ("New work, growth, enquiries, opportunities?")
- **D4:** Q5 + Q9 ("What do you still need to do?")

### For OUTCOMES (Employed Track)
- **D1:** Q5 ("How will skills equip you at work?")
- **D2:** Q6 ("Opportunities to implement skills")
- **D3:** Q7 ("Helping gain new role/responsibilities?")
- **D4:** Q5 + Q8 ("What do you still need to do?")

## Critical Rules for Scoring
1. **Never make causal claims.** Always frame analysis as "learners reported..."
2. **Only score complete pairs.** Do not score a learner if they only completed the baseline or only completed the outcomes profile.
3. **Dual-track learners.** Some learners did both the Employed and Entrepreneur reviews. These must be explicitly flagged and checked for which context better represents their goals before scoring.

# Layer 2 & Layer 3 Scoring Rubrics: Usage Reflection Arcs & Overall Bloom Ceiling

For evaluating the week-by-week "Usage Reflection" diaries (Layer 2) and establishing the learner's overall cognitive ceiling (Layer 3), we use a dual-framework approach.

## Layer 2: Weekly Sophistication Score (1-4)
Apply this score to **each individual response** to track how deeply AI is embedded in their practice *that particular week*. 
*(Note: Continue to extract text signals — e.g., tools named, bootcamp concepts used, impact language, and confidence register — alongside this 1-4 score).*

- **1: Basic / Passive.** The learner describes minimal, low-stakes, or personal AI use (holidays, recipes). Or they explicitly report not engaging with AI this week. 
- **2: Applied.** The learner uses AI for real, named professional tasks (e.g., drafting a report), but in a discrete, episodic, or "one-off" way rather than as an ongoing system.
- **3: Integrated.** AI is embedded in ongoing professional work. The learner describes repeatable workflows, custom tools in regular use, multi-task sessions, or continuing projects. 
- **4: Systemic.** AI operates at a system level. The learner is building tools for colleagues/clients, deploying multi-tool automated pipelines (e.g., Zapier to GPT to Email), or teaching AI skills to others.

## Layer 3: Overall Bloom's Taxonomy Ceiling (L1-L6)
Apply this score **once per learner** based on the *highest level of cognitive engagement evidenced at any point* across all their responses.

- **L1 (Remember):** Names tools and describes basic activities, but shows no evidence of understanding *why* AI works or applying it to professional tasks.
- **L2 (Understand):** Demonstrates conceptual understanding. Can explain *why* AI hallucinates, reflects on differences between tools, and accurately uses terms. 
- **L3 (Apply):** Consistently uses AI to complete real professional tasks with functional outputs. No evidence yet of system-building or critical iteration.
- **L4 (Analyse):** Breaks problems down. Compares tools using the same prompt, diagnoses *why* an AI failed, and critically reflects on AI accuracy/limitations.
	> *Note: "Strategic Learners" (managers delegating AI deployment for strategic oversight) may score L4 or L5 here based on systems-thinking and diagnosis, even if they aren't the ones typing the prompts.*
- **L5 (Create):** Designs and builds original AI-powered tools or systems (e.g., building a custom Gem, constructing an automation pipeline, curating a NotebookLM base).
- **L6 (Evaluate):** Tests systems, identifies failure points, and refines based on evidence. Cycles through build -> test -> identify problem -> adapt. Explicitly pre-verifies outputs before consequential use.
