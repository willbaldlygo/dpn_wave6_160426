### 2. Narrative Arc Synthesis

Learner 190's usage diary describes a highly accelerated journey from **Tactic to System Architect**.

The narrative arc moves through three distinct phases:

1.  **Foundational Adoption (Weeks 1-2):** Initially using AI for low-stakes, high-utility tasks like summarizing content for specific audiences and automating personal data entry.
2.  **Professional Systemization (Weeks 3-6):** Rapidly scaling use to corporate compliance, risk management, and standardized documentation across multiple ISO frameworks. The learner consistently moves beyond simple task completion to identify recurring business friction points and designing digital solutions (e.g., the evaluation forms, internal audit checklists) that *replace* manual effort.
3.  **Operational Deployment (Weeks 7-8):** The focus becomes building and testing robust, deployable systems. The learner demonstrates mastery of the AI ecosystem by comparing models (Gemini, CoPilot, etc.) and culminating in the development of a functional, multi-input operational application (the NCR App). The underlying theme is professional **productization**—transforming deep knowledge of a business process into an AI-powered, repeatable, marketable system.

### 3. Layer 3: Overall Bloom's Taxonomy Ceiling

**Overall Ceiling Score: L6 (Evaluate)**

**Reasoning:**
While the learner consistently demonstrates strong *Creation* skills (L5) by building the NCR App and designing the entire business structure/website, the presence of **L6 (Evaluate)** is justified by the critical nature of the problem-solving and the iterative, failure-aware approach taken throughout the diary.

1.  **System Testing and Refinement:** The learner is not just asking for an answer; they are *testing the boundaries* of the technology to see if it can handle the messy, variable nature of real-world business data (e.g., "struggled as there are tech issues," "what do you still need to do?", "hoping week 7 will help me to resolve some of my issues with getting complex formulas working properly"). This signals continuous **evaluation** of the tool's reliability against operational requirements.
2.  **Pre-Verification for Consequence:** In several instances (EPR data declaration, risk registers, client audits), the AI output is used as a robust *draft* that requires human oversight and cross-checking, proving the understanding that the AI output cannot be trusted 100%. This critical validation loop is the definition of evaluation.
3.  **Comparative Analysis:** The deliberate comparison and cross-pollination between multiple major AI tools (CoPilot, Claude, Gemini, ChatGPT) demonstrates an advanced ability to evaluate which tool is best suited for a specific functional requirement, moving beyond mere capability to optimization.

**Conclusion:** Learner 190 functions not merely as a prompt engineer, but as an **AI-Integrated Business Consultant** who is expert in evaluating the limitations and optimizing the strengths of AI across diverse, mission-critical business systems.
