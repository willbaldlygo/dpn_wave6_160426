Our Evaluation Framework

The dashboard quantifies subjective learner feedback into systematic metrics using a multi-layered rubric. This ensures consistent analysis across diverse cohorts and tracks cognitive development over time.

Layer 1

Learner Progression

Measures growth across 4 dimensions (Scored 1-3)

Adoption: Workflow integration depth.
Specificity: Concrete use-case detail.
Impact: Tangible business/work results.
Awareness: Critical/ethical understanding.
Layer 2

Weekly Sophistication

Measures active embedding (Scored 1-4)

1 Basic: Passive or accidental use.
2 Applied: One-off professional tasks.
3 Integrated: Repeatable workflows.
4 Systemic: Multi-tool automations.
Layer 3

Cognitive Ceiling

Bloom's Taxonomy adaptation (L1-L6)

L6 Evaluate: System testing & refinement.
L5 Create: Original tool/system architecture.
L4 Analyse: Critical tool diagnosis & comparison.
L3 Apply: Consistent functional outputs.
L2 Understand: Conceptual clarity on AI mechanics.
L1 Remember: Naming tools without depth.

The Objective

To systematically quantify string responses to understand if learners moved from mere awareness or early experimentation to active, embedded integration of AI tools in their daily work.

Layer 1: Dimensions (Scored 1-3)

  • AI Adoption Level: Workflow integration depth.
  • Specificity of Application: Concrete description detail.
  • Business/Work Impact: Tangible growth or measurable time.
  • AI Self-Awareness: Critical understanding / Human-in-loop.

Question Mapping

Outcomes (Entrepreneur): D1: Q5 | D2: Q7 | D3: Q8 | D4: Q5+9

Outcomes (Employed): D1: Q5 | D2: Q6 | D3: Q7 | D4: Q5+8

Critical Scoring Rules

  • Never make causal claims; frame as "learners reported."
  • Only score complete pairs (Baseline + Outcomes).
  • Flag dual-track learners before analysis to ensure correct context.
