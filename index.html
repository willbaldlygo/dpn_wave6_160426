Our Evaluation Framework

The dashboard quantifies subjective learner feedback into systematic metrics using a multi-layered rubric. This ensures consistent analysis across diverse cohorts and tracks cognitive development over time.

Layer 1

Learner Progression

Measures growth across 4 dimensions (Scored 1-3)

Adoption: Workflow integration depth.
Specificity: Concrete use-case detail.
Impact: Tangible business/work results.
Awareness: Critical/ethical understanding.

Weekly Sophistication

2 Applied: One-off professional tasks.

Cognitive Ceiling

Bloom's Taxonomy adaptation (L1-L6)

Layer 1 Scoring Rubric: Learner Progression

This rubric is used to rate learner development across the bootcamp based on their subjective, self-reported open-text responses.

The Objective

To systematically quantify string responses to understand if learners moved from mere awareness or early experimentation to active, embedded integration of AI tools in their daily work.

The Four Dimensions (Scored 1-3)

Dimension 1: AI Adoption Level

How embedded is AI in the learner's working life?

  • 1: No use, or heard of it but not using it.
  • 2: Occasional or experimental use of one or two tools.
  • 3: Regular use embedded in workflow; described as a working tool instead of a novel experiment.

Dimension 2: Specificity of Application

How concrete and detailed are the learner's descriptions of AI use?

  • 1: Vague intentions only (e.g., "I want to save time" or "I want to learn AI").
  • 2: Named tools or broad task types (e.g., "I use ChatGPT for emails").
  • 3: Concrete, implemented examples with specific use-cases (e.g., "I built an Airtable base linked to Zapier to automate client intake").

Dimension 3: Reported Business/Work Impact

Has AI produced tangible results in their work? (Note: Score as N/A at baseline)

  • 1: No impact described, or only hypothetical anticipated impact.
  • 2: General impact described (e.g., "it's saving me time" or "I feel more confident").
  • 3: Specific tangible impact named — business growth, revenue, or gain in new responsibilities.

Dimension 4: AI Self-Awareness

Does the learner show a critical understanding of how AI works?

  • 1: No evidence of critical understanding; treats AI as a black box.
  • 2: Shows a general understanding of AI's role and some basic limitations.
  • 3: Demonstrates nuanced understanding — human-in-the-loop, prompting techniques, and clarity on trust.

Critical Rules for Scoring

  • No Causal Claims: Always frame as "learners reported..."
  • Complete Pairs Only: Only score Baseline + Outcome pairs.
  • Dual-track Learners: Must be explicitly checked for context fit.

Layer 2 & 3: Reflections & Bloom Ceiling

Layer 2: Weekly Sophistication (1-4)

  • 1: Basic / Passive: Minimal, low-stakes, or personal AI use.
  • 2: Applied: Real professional tasks, but discrete or "one-off".
  • 3: Integrated: Embedded in ongoing work; repeatable workflows.
  • 4: Systemic: Building tools for others; multi-tool automated pipelines.

Layer 3: Overall Bloom's Taxonomy Ceiling (L1-L6)

  • L1 (Remember): Names tools and activities only.
  • L2 (Understand): Conceptual understanding of why AI works.
  • L3 (Apply): Professional tasks with functional outputs.
  • L4 (Analyse): Breaks problems down; tool comparison and diagnosis.
  • L5 (Create): Builds original AI tools or systems.
  • L6 (Evaluate): Tests, identifies failure points, and adapts systems.
